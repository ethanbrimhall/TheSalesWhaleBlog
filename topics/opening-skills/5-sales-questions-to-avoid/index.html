5 Sales Questions to Avoid

Published on February 26, 2019

Anyone who wants to be great at sales needs to master one skill: Asking great questions. The better you become at asking or needs assessment, the more you sell. Ask the right questions get the right answers.

Asking the wrong or inappropriate questions can crater your chances in a flash. Here are a few common daily questions that you need to avoid using with your prospects.

“What is more important to you, Price or Quality”?

If you are not the low-cost provider, you need to build value. Your customer will naturally associate a given amount of value with “what the product is supposed to cost” so a good salesperson will try to understand that perspective and discuss price from there. It’s all about Value.


“Are you the Decision Maker”?

Instead, learn about the customers decision making process. “who is involved in this process”? When you have connected with right way with you client you will have a complete understanding of their buying process.


“Can we get your husband, wife or significant other on the phone”?

This is hokey and old school. I have seen this play out in person once. NO Sale! If your prospect tells you they want to think it over are talk to _____ about it first (big lie), they are saying YOUR value did not match the price and/or you did not listen to what my needs were. That’s your problem; fix the selling mistake and then you don’t need hokey gimmicks.


“Will you be getting other quotes”?

That’s a great idea, encourage your customer to get other quotes due to your lack of confidence. You have the best product, service and value in town...now you have to prove it. If you listened and hit your mark, you don’t have to worry about competition. You are on your own level.


“What can I do to earn your business”?

This question also implies that you will take on anyone and are willing to be insincere to close a deal. Now at this point your just an order taker. Spend time knowing your customer, build solutions that are lasting. If it’s not meant to be then it’s time to move on to the next prospect.

“Sometimes the questions are complicated and the answers are simple.”
- Dr. Seuss

Aim high today and always give your best! - TL
